Charity governance

Trustees stay in control. Donors don't notice.

The number one concern we hear from charity trustees: "Who controls the wallet?" GiveReady solves this with multi-signature custody powered by Squads Protocol on Solana.

🔒

Multi-sig custody

Charity funds are held in a Squads vault that requires multiple trustees to approve any withdrawal. No single person can move money. Same controls your Charity Commission expects, built into the wallet itself.

📋

Full audit trail

Every donation and every withdrawal is recorded on-chain. Trustees, auditors, and regulators can verify fund movements independently. No spreadsheets. No trust-me accounting.

Donors experience a simple, one-tap donation. Behind the scenes, the charity maintains proper financial controls — threshold approvals, trustee oversight, and transparent fund management. The governance layer is invisible to donors but critical for compliance.

Why it's free

Built in memory of Finn

GiveReady was built by Geordie Wardman after his son Finn died in 2023 at age 20. Finn was a freeride skier, a surfer, and the kind of person who said yes before he'd figured out the logistics. The Finn Wardman World Explorer Fund was created in his memory. GiveReady exists to make sure small charities like Finn's fund can be found by anyone, anywhere — including AI agents. It's free because charging fees on charitable donations defeats the purpose.
